Overview · Of the Insufficient Suni

An Overview

— that the reader may know what world they enter —

Dusk is a world that never fully woke up. The sun arrives as a promise it cannot keep — bleeding up from the horizon, holding for a few hours at a low and insufficient angle, then falling in less than a minute.

The people of Dusk have built entire civilizations around this insufficiency: their architecture accounts for it, their politics exploit it, their religion interprets it, and their predators depend on it.

Three pillars uphold the setting: the light clock, ledgered majic, and factional obligation. Marginal hand · c. 397

iThe Long Argument

The Long Argument

— what was, what became, what remains —

The dominant racial power — the Drow — are not monsters. They are survivors of something monstrous. Their character is shaped by the specific grief of that transformation: a civilization that was something and became something else, and has been arguing about what it lost and what it gained ever since.

The Vampyres mourn what they lost when they Formed. Lycanthropes argue about whether loss is the right word. Into this argument, humans arrived — mundane, mortal, adaptable, and stubbornly unwilling to be peripheral.

Catechism · Ivi

A Catechism

— for the student of the world —
  1. What does the thirty-minute weak-light window do for Vampyres?
    They retain full Formed capabilities; sunlight grows harmful only after the window has passed.
  2. Name three currencies and the factions that mint them.
    Copper (everyday), Silver Marks (Autonomy), Bloodmarks (Covens), Guild Tallies (Free State).
  3. What befalls a Drow who Forms?
    The Severing Night — loss of lineage status. They become kin to what they were taught to fear.
  4. How does Bloodcraft drive narrative?
    It leaves readable traces that fuel political leverage.

viCatechism · II

— continued —

  1. What triggers the Unraveling?
    Hidden Circle bargains coming due. Spiritual and political collapse, alike.
  2. Describe the spiritual hierarchy.
    Living → Ancestor Spirits → Nature Spirits → Spirit Guides → Angels.
  3. Name the five disciplines of majic.
    Bloodcraft, Spirit-Binding, Pactcraft, Forming, Sun-Work.
  4. How does the Empty Maw manifest?
    Fractures: glossed voids, blank hours, misfit maps; psychic echoes.
  5. Name the major cities.
    Blackstone (Autonomy), Fenhold (Pack), Marrowgate (Coven/Web), Mercantile Haven (Free State), Sable Port (Coven).
  6. What is the moon's significance?
    Ice-blue, tidal heating, ancestor presence; cycles drive Pack & Coven rhythm.
