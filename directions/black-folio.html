I

Overview

That the reader may know
what world they enter

Dusk is a world that never fully woke up. The sun arrives as a promise it cannot keep — bleeding up from the horizon, holding for a few hours at a low and insufficient angle, then falling in less than a minute.

The people of Dusk have built entire civilizations around this insufficiency: their architecture accounts for it, their politics exploit it, their religion interprets it, and their predators depend on it.

The dominant racial power — the Drow — are not monsters. They are survivors of something monstrous that happened to their ancestors. Their character is shaped by the specific grief of that transformation: a civilization that was something and became something else, and has been arguing about what it lost and what it gained ever since.

The Vampyres mourn what they lost when they Formed. Lycanthropes argue about whether loss is the right word. Into this argument, humans arrived — mundane, mortal, adaptable, and stubbornly unwilling to be peripheral.

III

Mechanics

At the table,
by the candle
Of the Light Cycle— measured in minutes —
Black Hour
The 20–30 minutes before sunrise. Undead at full capability; daylight not yet a defense.
Sunrise
A 5–10 minute ascent. Soft transition; rituals time to it.
Transition Window
0–30 min. Formed retain capability. Pacts settle. The dangerous half-hour.
Daylight
2–5 hours by season and region. Highland delay, forest canopy, Basin fog, Snowfields zero-window.
Sunset
Under one minute. The drop. Plans live and die in this window.
Of the Five Disciplines— and their Transactional Principle —
Bloodcraft
Ledger and pool. Mass Read; Red-Iron Blade. Leaves traces that fuel political leverage.
Spirit-Binding
Negotiation, emergency binding. Yields Spirit-Scars; some never close.
Pactcraft
Demonic investments. Compounding cost. The Hidden Circle's instrument.
Forming
Thresholds. Deliberate vs. involuntary crossings; each with its grief.
Sun-Work
Only within the window. Draws demonic attention; rarely undertaken alone.

Of the Powers

Each holds the world
by a different throat
  • Drow Autonomy
    Lineage · Law · Matriarchate
     Inquisition and Web intelligence. The argument that became a state. Holds the deep cities and the registries that say who you are.
  • The Covens
    Vampyre Houses · Blood-Archives
     Pale-Remembrance keeps the long ledger. Marrowgate is theirs. A Coven debt outlives its debtor.
  • The Packs
    Lycanthropic Great-Houses
     Hunt Courts and Pack-Rebirth. Fenhold runs by their teeth. The moon is their loudest court.
  • The Free State
    Neutral Commerce · Guild Tallies
     Mercantile Haven. The road every predator must use. Tallies are its tongue and sword.
  • The Web
    Memory-Binding · Dossiers
     Knows what you forgot you said. Covert operations. Exists chiefly in margins of other factions' ledgers.
  • The Hidden Circle
    Demonic Investments · Unraveling
     The bargain that compounds. The cause everyone has reason to deny. The reason the present is brittle.
VI

A Catechism

For the student
of the world
  • What does the thirty-minute weak-light window do for Vampyres?
    They retain full Formed capabilities; sunlight grows harmful only after the window has passed.
  • Name three currencies and the factions that mint them.
    Copper (everyday), Silver Marks (Autonomy), Bloodmarks (Covens), Guild Tallies (Free State).
  • What befalls a Drow who Forms?
    The Severing Night — loss of lineage status. They become kin to what they were taught to fear.
  • How does Bloodcraft drive narrative?
    It leaves readable traces that fuel political leverage. Nothing done in blood is done in private.
  • What triggers the Unraveling?
    Hidden Circle bargains coming due. Spiritual and political collapse, alike.
  • Name the spiritual hierarchy.
    Living → Ancestor Spirits → Nature Spirits → Spirit Guides → Angels.
  • Name the five disciplines of majic.
    Bloodcraft, Spirit-Binding, Pactcraft, Forming, Sun-Work.
