Chapter the First

An Overview

— that the reader may know what world they enter —

Dusk is a world that never fully woke up. The sun arrives as a promise it cannot keep — bleeding up from the horizon, holding for a few hours at a low and insufficient angle, then falling in less than a minute. The people of Dusk have built entire civilizations around this insufficiency: their architecture accounts for it, their politics exploit it, their religion interprets it, and their predators depend on it.

Three pillars uphold the setting: the light clock, ledgered majic, and factional obligation. Marginal hand, c. 397

The dominant racial power — the Drow — are not monsters. They are survivors of something monstrous that happened to their ancestors, and their current character is shaped by the specific grief of that transformation: a civilization that was something and became something else, and has been arguing about what it lost and what it gained ever since.

The Vampyres mourn what they lost when they Formed. Lycanthropes argue about whether loss is the right word. Drow look at both and cannot agree whether they are looking at their own future or their own past. Into this argument, humans arrived — mundane, mortal, adaptable, and stubbornly unwilling to be peripheral.

The Hidden Circle's demonic bargains drive the present crisis, called the Unraveling. Do not study Dusk without studying its debt.

Power is divided among the Drow Autonomy, the Covens, the Packs, the Free State, the Web, and independent shamans. Each holds part of the world by the throat; none holds it all.

"We did not lose the sun. We were given just enough of it to remember what we are missing."
Chapter the Second

Themes & Topics

— upon which the campaign turns —

Gothic Intimacy

Horror is personal. Relationships are leverage and lifeline both. The monster is the cousin who returned changed.

Political Horror

Survival is political. Affiliation determines whose door opens, whose blade is sheathed, whose ledger forgives.

Ritual & Obligation

Deals are binding. Spirits enforce promises. A word spoken under blood is heavier than a word spoken under steel.

The Cost of Purity

The Forming's grief shapes Drow society. Lineage is both inheritance and indictment.

Form Over Blood

What you became matters more than what you were born. A peasant who Forms eclipses a noble who did not.

The Empty Maw

Absence as ontological threat. Glossed voids, blank hours, misfit maps. The dark that is not just dark.

Chapter the Fifth

Of the Powers

— each holds the world by a different throat —

Drow Autonomy

Lineage · Law · Matriarchate

Inquisition and Web intelligence. The argument that became a state. Holds the deep cities and the registries that say who you are.

The Covens

Vampyre Houses · Blood-Archives

Pale-Remembrance keeps the long ledger. Marrowgate is theirs. A Coven debt outlives its debtor.

The Packs

Lycanthropic Great-Houses

Hunt Courts and Pack-Rebirth. Fenhold runs by their teeth. The moon is their loudest court.

The Free State

Neutral Commerce · Guild Tallies

Mercantile Haven. The road that all three predators must use. Tallies are its tongue and its sword.

The Web

Memory-Binding · Dossiers

Knows what you forgot you said. Covert operations. The faction that exists chiefly in margins of other factions' ledgers.

The Hidden Circle

Demonic Investments · Unraveling

The bargain that compounds. The cause everyone has reason to deny. The reason the present is brittle.

Chapter the Sixth

A Catechism

— for the student of the world —
  1. What does the thirty-minute weak-light window do for Vampyres?They retain full Formed capabilities; sunlight grows harmful only after the window has passed.
  2. Name three currencies and the factions that mint them.Copper (everyday), Silver Marks (Autonomy), Bloodmarks (Covens), Guild Tallies (Free State).
  3. What befalls a Drow who Forms?The Severing Night — loss of lineage status. They become kin to what they were taught to fear.
  4. How does Bloodcraft drive narrative?It leaves readable traces that fuel political leverage. Nothing done in blood is done in private.
  5. What triggers the Unraveling?Hidden Circle bargains coming due. Spiritual and political collapse alike.
  6. Describe the spiritual hierarchy.Living → Ancestor Spirits → Nature Spirits → Spirit Guides → Angels.
  7. Name the five disciplines of majic.Bloodcraft, Spirit-Binding, Pactcraft, Forming, Sun-Work.
Chapter the Seventh

The Study

— the reader's own ledger —
  • Memorise the weak-light window and its regional variations.
  • Learn lunar and tidal cycles, and the alignment at the peaks.
  • Understand Forming and its racial implications.
  • Review all five majic disciplines and the Transactional Principle.
  • Know the key factions, their NPCs, and the lines between them.
  • Master the economy: currencies, formulae, trade.
  • Study settlements city by city, town by town.
  • Grasp the spiritual hierarchy and the shaman's neutrality.
  • Understand the Empty Maw and its fractures.
