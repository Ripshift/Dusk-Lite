Dusk — Campaign Sourcebook

Study Guide & Quick Reference

Overview

Dusk is a compact gothic roleplaying setting built around three pillars: the light clock, ledgered majic, and factional obligation. The world's weak red sun and ice‑blue moon shape travel, combat, and ritual. Power is divided among the Drow Autonomy, Covens, Packs, the Free State, the Web, and independent shamans. The Hidden Circle's demonic bargains drive the crisis known as the Unraveling.

Dusk is a world that never fully woke up. The sun arrives as a promise it cannot keep — bleeding up from the horizon, holding for a few hours at a low and insufficient angle, then falling in less than a minute. The people of Dusk have built entire civilizations around this insufficiency: their architecture accounts for it, their politics exploit it, their religion interprets it, and their predators depend on it.

The dominant racial power — the Drow — are not monsters. They are survivors of something monstrous that happened to their ancestors, and their current character is shaped by the specific grief of that transformation: a civilization that was something and became something else, and that has been arguing about what it lost and what it gained ever since. The Vampyres mourn what they lost when they Formed. Lycanthropes argue about whether loss is the right word. Drow look at both and cannot agree whether they are looking at their own future or their own past.

Into this argument, humans arrived — mundane, mortal, adaptable, and stubbornly unwilling to be peripheral. They did not receive the question the same way the Drow did, and this makes them either the most useful allies in the world or its most dangerous wildcards.

Main Topics & Themes

Light as a Game Clock

  • 30‑minute weak‑light window after sunrise; sunset lasts under a minute.
  • Moon phases (15‑day cycle) and tidal month (30 days) affect Packs and Covens.
  • Regional variations: Highland delay, forest canopy, Basin fog, Snowfields zero-window.

Factions & Power

  • Drow Autonomy: lineage, law, Matriarchate, Inquisition, Web intelligence.
  • Covens: vampyre houses, blood‑archives, Pale‑Remembrance.
  • Packs: lycanthropic great‑houses, Hunt Courts, Pack‑Rebirth.
  • Free State: neutral commerce, Guild Tallies.
  • Web: memory‑binding, dossiers, covert operations.
  • Hidden Circle: demonic investments causing the Unraveling.
  • Shamans: neutrality imperative, cross-faction access.

Core Themes

  • Gothic Intimacy: Horror is personal, relationships are key.
  • Political Horror: Survival is political, affiliation matters.
  • Ritual and Obligation: Deals are binding, spirits enforce promises.
  • The Cost of Purity: Forming's grief shapes Drow society.
  • Form Over Blood: What you became matters more than birth.

World History

  • Pale-Elf Period: Surface-oriented, demon-contact points.
  • Interregnum: Post-blight chaos.
  • Current Age: 400-year stability, Hidden Circle instability.

Quick Reference Mechanics

Light Cycle

  • Pre-Dawn (Black Hour): 20-30 min before sunrise.
  • Sunrise: 5-10 min ascent.
  • Transition Window: 0-30 min; undead active, Formed retain capabilities.
  • Daylight: 2-5 hours; varies by season/region.
  • Sunset: <1 min drop.

Moon & Tide

  • Lunar Cycle: 15 days; Full Moon (Day 8) peaks Pack activity.
  • Tidal Month: 30 days; Stages A-F; Stage A coincides with Full Moon.
  • Alignment: Lunar and tidal cycles sync at peaks.

Forming

  • Vampyre: Surveillance, action, rogue, Elder sanction.
  • Lycanthrope: Escalation sequences, Pack cohesion.
  • Silver: No regeneration, pain amplification.
  • Spiritual Sensitivity: Ancestor spirits, wights/revenants.

Majic

  • Bloodcraft: Ledger, blood pool, operations (Mass Read, Red-Iron Blade).
  • Spirit-Binding: Negotiation, emergency binding.
  • Pactcraft: Investments, demonic bargains.
  • Forming: Thresholds, deliberate vs. involuntary.
  • Sun-Work: Light window, demonic attention.
  • Transactional Principle: Ledger balance, Spirit-Scars.

Economy & Trade

  • Currency: Copper, Silver Marks, Bloodmarks, Guild Tallies.
  • Price Formula: Base × (1 + 0.1 × (S + D + R)).
  • Caravan: Logistics, smuggling.
  • Information Economy: Knowledge as currency.

Combat & Arms

  • Arms by Tier: Autonomy (formal), Free State (mercenary), Lower Class (improvised).
  • Bloodcraft/Sun-Work in Combat: Ledger manipulation, light manipulation.
  • Morale: Faction-specific, Pack cohesion.

Practice Questions

  1. What does the 30‑minute weak‑light window do for Vampyres?
    They retain full Formed capabilities; sunlight becomes harmful after 30 minutes.
  2. Name three currencies and their factions.
    Copper (everyday), Silver Marks (Autonomy), Bloodmarks (Covens), Guild Tallies (Free State).
  3. What happens when a Drow undergoes Forming?
    They undergo Severing Night—loss of lineage status.
  4. How does Bloodcraft drive narrative?
    It leaves readable traces that fuel political leverage.
  5. What triggers the Unraveling?
    Hidden Circle bargains coming due, causing spiritual and political collapse.
  6. Describe the spiritual hierarchy in Dusk.
    Living → Ancestor Spirits → Nature Spirits → Spirit Guides → Angels.
  7. What are the five disciplines of majic?
    Bloodcraft, Spirit-Binding, Pactcraft, Forming, Sun-Work.
  8. How does the Empty Maw manifest?
    Fractures: glossed voids, blank hours, misfit maps; psychic echoes.
  9. Name the major cities and their controlling factions.
    Blackstone (Autonomy), Fenhold (Pack/Naga), Marrowgate (Coven/Web), Mercantile Haven (Free State), Sable Port (Coven).
  10. What is the significance of the moon in Dusk?
    Ice-blue, tidal heating, spiritual significance as ancestor presence.

Key Takeaways

  • Timing is everything: light, moon, tide shape all activities.
  • Documents and blood shape politics: lineage papers, blood-compacts.
  • Majic is transactional and traceable: ledger balance, Spirit-Scars.
  • Factions are interdependent and unstable: leverage points, obligation networks.
  • Dusk rewards investigation and consequence‑driven play.
  • Gothic intimacy: Horror is personal, relationships key.
  • Political horror: Survival is political, affiliation matters.
  • Ritual obligation: Spirits enforce promises.
  • Cost of purity: Forming's grief shapes society.
  • Form over blood: What you became matters more than birth.
  • Empty Maw: Absence as ontological threat.
  • World history: Pale-elf legacy, Shattering, current instability.

Study Checklist

  • Memorize the weak‑light window and regional variations.
  • Learn lunar and tidal cycles, including alignments.
  • Understand Forming mechanics and racial dynamics.
  • Review all majic disciplines and transactional principle.
  • Know key factions, NPCs, and their relationships.
  • Master economy: currencies, price formula, trade mechanics.
  • Study settlements: cities, towns, villages by region.
  • Grasp religion: spiritual hierarchy, ancestor worship, shamans.
  • Understand the Empty Maw: manifestations, faction responses.
  • Review world: planet, climate, history, cosmology.
